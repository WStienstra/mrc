# MRC (Mandelbrot Reference Coding) — Adversarial Prior-Art Memo

Date: 2026-06-10
Scope: novelty attack on the four claim elements: (1) zero-byte, math-regenerated codebook; (2) approximate match + correction residual; (3) multi-scale codebook bank; (4) sub-KB payload effectiveness vs zstd-with-dictionary.

---

## 1. Executive verdict: **AT RISK**

Not cleanly knocked out, but **the broad mechanism is anticipated**. A granted (now expired) US patent — **US 7,184,597 B1 (Aronov, 2007)** — discloses lossless compression in which a dictionary is generated by a random number generator, is *never transmitted*, is *regenerated by the decoder from a tiny parameter protocol*, and where the encoder transmits *(dictionary index + least-distance correction)*. That is claim elements (1) and (2) at the generic level, in one reference. Separately, ACELP's "algebraic codebook" (deployed worldwide since the 1990s in G.729/AMR) is textbook prior art for "codebook defined by a mathematical rule, never stored or transmitted," and relative-entropy-coding / minimal-random-coding literature (2019–2026) explicitly uses shared-PRNG-regenerated codebooks with index-only transmission.

**What survives:** nothing we found uses *Mandelbrot/Julia escape-time orbit data specifically* as the codebook source, nothing combines an *input-independent* mathematically regenerated codebook with *lossless residual correction* and a *multi-scale bank*, and nothing targets the sub-KB regime against trained-dictionary baselines. A patent is plausible only with **narrow claims** (Section 4). A broad claim on "regenerate the codebook from a seed instead of transmitting it" will be rejected under §102 (Aronov) or §103 (Aronov + ACELP/REC), and an examiner who finds Aronov will likely cite it as primary art. Budget accordingly: this is a narrow-improvement filing, not a pioneer filing.

## 3. Detailed analysis per reference

### 3.1 US 7,184,597 B1 — Aronov (NEAR-KNOCKOUT)
- **Cite:** "Method of compression of binary data with a random number generator," Edward Lasar Aronov, filed 2003-12-23, granted 2007-02-27, **expired 2024-04-09 (fee-related)**. https://patents.google.com/patent/US7184597B1/en
- **Mechanism:** An RNG generates a "compressing dictionary" whose amplitude/frequency distribution is shaped to resemble the input. Encoder finds, for each input sample, the dictionary entry at least distance (via a content-addressable-memory search), and outputs *(least distances, dictionary indexes, protocol)*, where the protocol is the small set of RNG parameters. Specification: *"The compressing dictionary is not a part of the output data... it can be reproduced any time for decompression with the protocol."* Explicitly lossless: *"All operations of compression... were reversible... the process of decompression is lossless."*
- **Reads on us:** regenerated-not-transmitted dictionary (claim element 1, generically), approximate match + correction residual (element 2), lossless. This is the single most dangerous reference. As an expired patent it is pure prior art (no FTO issue).
- **Distinguishing hooks:** (a) Aronov's dictionary is **input-adaptive** — RNG parameters are tuned to the input's frequency distribution and transmitted per message; MRC's codebook is **input-independent**, fixed universal constants, identical for every message and every deployment. (b) Aronov uses a generic PRNG sample stream; MRC uses structured Mandelbrot orbit/escape-time data with claimed multi-scale self-similar structure. (c) No multi-scale bank. (d) No small-payload/IoT framing. Expect a §103 fight rather than §102 only if claims recite (a)+(b) explicitly.

### 3.2 ACELP / CELP codebooks (PARTIAL — element 1 is 30+ years old)
- **Cite:** Algebraic Code-Excited Linear Prediction, Adoul & Laflamme et al. (Univ. Sherbrooke/VoiceAge), standardized in ITU-T G.729 (1996), G.723.1, AMR, AMR-WB; Wikipedia: the fixed codebook *"is represented by an algebraic formula or algorithm, whereby there is no need to store the whole codebook, but only the algorithm."* Predecessor: CELP's stochastic codebook of Gaussian vectors (Schroeder & Atal, ICASSP 1985) — fixed pseudo-random codebook known to both sides, index+gain transmitted.
- **Reads on us:** "deterministic, never-stored, never-transmitted codebook + transmit index + minimize residual error" is the operating principle of essentially every phone call since ~1996. An examiner combining ACELP (algebraic codebook) with any lossless residual coder gets close to claims 1+2 by obviousness.
- **Distinguishing hooks:** lossy, speech-specific, codebook is sparse pulse patterns (not content snippets), residual is minimized not transmitted-for-lossless-reconstruction, no general-purpose data claims, no multi-scale bank.

### 3.3 Relative entropy coding / minimal random coding (PARTIAL)
- **Cite:** Havasi, Peharz, Hernández-Lobato, "Minimal Random Code Learning," ICLR 2019; Flamich et al., "Compressing Images by Encoding Their Latent Representations with Relative Entropy Coding," NeurIPS 2020; Flamich PhD thesis (Cambridge 2024); Flamich & Gündüz, "Data Compression with Stochastic Codes," arXiv:2602.07635 (2026); survey arXiv:2506.16309.
- **Mechanism:** Encoder and decoder share a public PRNG seed; both draw the same M samples from a shared prior (i.e., regenerate an identical codebook with zero transmitted bytes); encoder transmits only the index of the selected sample. Note the embarrassing acronym collision: this technique is literally called **MRC** (minimal random coding) in the literature.
- **Distinguishing hooks:** lossy/stochastic (encodes a *sample*, not exact data), codebook is random not structured-fractal, no correction residual for losslessness, no multi-scale bank. But it firmly establishes "both sides regenerate the codebook from a seed; transmit only an address" as known art from 2019.

### 3.4 πfs (PARTIAL, informal but citable)
- **Cite:** philipl/pifs, "πfs — the data-free filesystem!", GitHub (≈2012, forks jmaher/Nule/ajeetdsouza), HN thread id=36357466 (2023). https://github.com/philipl/pifs
- **Mechanism:** Stores every file as (index, length) into the digits of π, computed on demand via the Bailey–Borwein–Plouffe formula — i.e., both sides "regenerate" the reference data from a mathematical constant; nothing but addresses are stored. Satire: the index is on average as large as the data (its own issue #57 documents the no-compression argument).
- **Reads on us:** the *concept* of "address into a deterministically computable mathematical object instead of shipping data" is publicly disclosed. Informal disclosure counts. It also flags the **information-theoretic objection examiners/reviewers will raise**: addresses into a content-independent codebook cannot beat entropy on average; MRC's pitch must be that real telemetry blocks are close (in residual-energy terms) to orbit-data structure, making *address+small residual* shorter than raw — that's an empirical claim, so keep benchmark data in the spec.
- **Distinguishing hooks:** no approximate matching, no residual, no codebook bank, no practicality.

### 3.5 Lattice / trellis-coded quantization (PARTIAL)
- **Cite:** US 5,297,170 "Lattice and trellis-coded quantization" (Eyuboglu/Forney, 1994); US 6,728,413 (lattice VQ in image compression); Conway & Sloane lattice quantizer literature (1980s).
- **Mechanism:** Codebook = points of an algebraic lattice; nearest-neighbor computed by formula; codebook never stored or transmitted. Long-standing answer to "VQ without codebook storage."
- **Distinguishing hooks:** lossy quantizer; codepoints are geometric, not content-bearing snippets; no residual-corrected lossless mode in the MRC sense; no fractal generation.

### 3.6 Jacquin/Barnsley fractal coding (PARTIAL)
- **Cite:** US 4,941,193 (Barnsley & Sloan, 1990); US 5,065,447 (Barnsley, 1991, PIFS); Jacquin, "Image coding based on a fractal theory of iterated contractive image transformations," IEEE Trans. Image Proc., 1992; survey: Fisher / ACM Computing Surveys "A Review of the Fractal Image Compression Literature." Hybrids with explicit fixed VQ codebooks exist (IEEE 821730, 559456) but those codebooks are **trained by clustering**, not mathematically generated.
- **Key adversarial finding:** we searched specifically for any fractal-coding variant using a *fixed mathematically generated* codebook (rather than the image's own domain pool or a trained VQ codebook) and **found none**. The "codebook" in fractal coding is explicitly described in the literature as "virtual... domain blocks are taken from the image itself."
- **Distinguishing hooks:** self-referential, image-derived, lossy. The MRC name invites confusion with this art; the spec should expressly distinguish "fractal compression" (IFS, self-similarity of the *input*) from MRC (fractal-*generated reference data*, input plays no role in codebook construction).

### 3.7 Sun et al. 2015, fractal dictionary + Julia set (DISTINGUISHABLE)
- **Cite:** Y. Sun, R. Xu, L. Chen, X. Hu, "Image compression and encryption scheme using fractal dictionary and Julia set," IET Image Processing 9(3):173–183, 2015. https://ietresearch.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1049/iet-ipr.2014.0224
- **Mechanism:** Fractal-dictionary encoding accelerates fractal image coding; separately, **Julia set parameters seed a random sequence used as encryption keys** (scrambled via Hilbert curve) for a stream cipher over the compressed data.
- **Verdict:** Closest Mandelbrot-family publication, but the Julia set supplies *cryptographic key material*, not codebook content; the compression dictionary is image-derived. Distinguishable — yet cite it in the IDS; an examiner will find it on the same keywords.

### 3.8 US 11,012,525 (Salesforce IoT) and US 11,967,975 (seed bits) — DISTINGUISHABLE
- 11,012,525: both endpoints build identical dictionaries *from the observed data stream* without exchanging them (version IDs/checksums only). Solves the same problem (dictionary overhead on IoT links) by adaptation, not regeneration-from-math. Distinguishable, but it owns the "IoT, dictionary never transmitted" framing — claim language must not read on synchronized *learned* dictionaries. **Active patent — also an FTO data point.**
- 11,967,975: rules+seed recursive decomposition; the rules (dictionary) are extracted from the data and shipped. Distinguishable. **Active to 2042 — FTO check on its claims advisable, novelty threat low.**

### 3.9 Tacconelli 2026 (Fibonacci hierarchies) — PARTIAL, timing-sensitive
- **Cite:** R. Tacconelli, "Aperiodic Structures Never Collapse: Fibonacci Hierarchies for Lossless Compression," arXiv:2603.14999 (March 2026).
- **Mechanism:** A deterministic, mathematically defined aperiodic hierarchy (phrase lengths 2,3,5,8,...,144) that both encoder and decoder regenerate without transmission, used to structure dictionary positions for lossless compression ("Quasicryth"; ~20.7 MB saved on enwik9).
- **Verdict:** PARTIAL — math-regenerated *structure* (positions/phrase lengths), not math-generated codebook *content*. **Action: confirm whether its arXiv date precedes our priority date; if we have no filing yet, it is prior art; if filed before March 2026, it isn't.**

### 3.10 Brotli/zstd/SDCH (CONTEXT)
- Brotli (RFC 7932) ships a 120 KB static dictionary *inside the spec* — zero dictionary bytes per message, exactly the deployment property MRC advertises; it is trained from a web corpus and stored, not regenerated. zstd `--train` (Cover/FastCover) is the acknowledged sub-KB SOTA and the correct benchmark baseline. SDCH (Google, 2008, deprecated) = negotiated shared dictionaries. None regenerate from math; all are the "established art" backdrop. Implication for marketing and claims: the differentiator is **no stored dictionary anywhere** (≈30 B of constants + generator code), not "no dictionary bytes on the wire."

### 3.11 Searched and NOT found (the dog that didn't bark)
- No publication, patent, or forum post using **Mandelbrot/Julia escape-time or orbit data as compression codebook content**. Searches of Google Patents, arXiv, IEEE/ACM surfacing, HN, fractalforums, reddit returned: Mandelbrot *image* compression (RLE of renders), Mandelbrot-based *encryption*, fractal *coding of images* — never "Mandelbrot data as universal reference dictionary."
- No "multi-scale bank of mathematically regenerated codebooks" anywhere.
- Chaotic-map literature (logistic map etc.) uses chaos for *encryption/shuffling* of VQ codebooks, not for generating codebook content both sides regenerate (one 1993 paper, Torres & Arias, "Stochastic generation of the codebook" — paywalled, title suggests random codebook initialization for training; obtain via library before filing).

## 4. Recommended claim language that survives the closest art

Do **not** claim (anticipated): "compressing data by transmitting an index into a codebook that the decoder regenerates from a seed" (Aronov §102; ACELP/REC §103) or "...plus a correction residual" (Aronov §102).

**Independent claim skeleton (survives Aronov/ACELP/REC/πfs as a combination):**

> A lossless compression method comprising:
> (a) at both an encoder and a decoder, deterministically generating an identical **bank of codebooks at a plurality of block scales**, wherein each codebook entry is derived from **orbit trajectories of an iterated complex quadratic map z ← z² + c** evaluated at parameter points specified by a **fixed, input-independent set of constants of at most ~tens of bytes**, the constants being identical for all messages and independent of any statistics of the data to be compressed;
> (b) at the encoder, selecting for each input block a scale and an entry address minimizing a distortion metric, and entropy-coding **(scale, address, correction residual)** such that the decoder's output is bit-exact;
> (c) at the decoder, regenerating said bank from the same constants and reconstructing each block as codebook entry ⊕ residual.

Load-bearing limitations and why:
1. **"input-independent / identical for all messages"** — defeats Aronov (his RNG protocol is tuned per input and transmitted) and US 11,012,525 (learned in-flight).
2. **"orbit trajectories of an iterated complex quadratic map"** (or broader: "escape-time dynamics of an iterated nonlinear complex map") — defeats generic-PRNG art (Aronov, CELP stochastic codebook, REC) and lattice VQ; this is the genuinely unoccupied ground. Consider a fallback dependent claim at "iterated nonlinear dynamical system" breadth and expect it to be contested.
3. **"bank ... at a plurality of block scales" with joint (scale, address) coding** — no found reference has this.
4. **"lossless / bit-exact via entropy-coded correction residual"** — defeats ACELP, lattice VQ, REC (all lossy).
5. Dependent claims: residual-energy-based scale selection; sub-1 KB payload application (LPWAN/satellite telemetry); codebook materialization cache on constrained decoders; constants published as a versioned profile ID.

Also: (i) put the zstd-dictionary benchmark numbers in the spec — utility/non-obviousness argument; (ii) pre-empt the πfs/counting objection in the spec (explain why address+residual beats raw on the target distribution); (iii) IDS should include every reference in Section 2 — burying Aronov would risk inequitable-conduct exposure, and it's expired anyway so it costs nothing to distinguish honestly.

**Honest obviousness assessment:** the realistic examiner rejection is *Aronov (regenerated RNG dictionary + index + residual, lossless) in view of ACELP (algebraic/structured codebook generation) and/or Sun 2015 (fractal-parameter-generated reference sequences)*, arguing it's obvious to swap Aronov's generator for a fractal one. The rebuttal must be technical: Mandelbrot orbit data provides *structured, multi-scale, self-similar* entries that materially change residual statistics versus white PRNG output — that argument needs experimental evidence (ablation: MRC codebook vs same-size PRNG codebook vs same-size lattice, residual entropy compared). **Without that ablation in hand, expect the §103 rejection to stick.**
